# Seed Note: AoA Session Donor Harvest

## Purpose

Preserve one reviewed-session donor harvest bundle that proposes a new scaffold
skill, `aoa-session-donor-harvest`, while explicitly keeping
`aoa-quest-harvest` narrow.

This note does not open a new wave, replace the current
`navigation.next_live_seed`, or treat a session-derived bundle as if it were
already canonical `aoa-skills` truth.
It exists to keep the bundle bounded, replayable, and owner-aware until one
later planting intentionally chooses it.

## Why this belongs in Dionysus

`Dionysus` is the seed garden and dispatch layer.
This donor note belongs here because:

- the bundle is a session-derived donor harvest, not yet an owner-repo source
- the proposed first owner is `aoa-skills`, but later lineage also touches
  `aoa-techniques`
- the bundle needs an explicit decision about whether pending technique lineage
  may stay pending in a scaffold landing
- the current live seed order should remain untouched until one later bounded
  planting chooses this route

## Bundle contents to preserve

The source artifact is `archive/seed_pack_exports/aoa-session-donor-harvest.zip`.

It carries:

- `aoa-session-donor-harvest-deliverables.md`
- `aoa-session-donor-harvest-recommendation.md`
- `aoa-session-donor-harvest-source.zip`
- `aoa-session-donor-harvest.patch`
- `aoa-session-donor-harvest-patch.zip`
- `aoa-session-donor-harvest-codex-portable.zip`

The bundle proposes:

- a new scaffold skill `aoa-session-donor-harvest`
- a narrow boundary clarification for `aoa-quest-harvest`
- `aoa-skills` config, fixture, and portable-export additions that would make
  the new skill routable and testable inside that owner repo

## Ownership posture

Read this donor note under these boundaries:

- `aoa-skills` is the first honest owner for the proposed scaffold skill bundle
- `aoa-techniques` is the later home for published technique lineage if the
  pending technique IDs become real canon
- `Dionysus` only preserves the donor bundle and its owner-choice questions
- `aoa-quest-harvest` remains a narrower final promotion-triage skill and
  should not quietly absorb upstream donor extraction
- `aoa-routing` and `aoa-kag` are derivative layers and should not become the
  first authoring home for this meaning

## What this bundle is for

- reviewed-session donor extraction
- owner-layer routing across technique, skill, playbook, eval, memo, agent, and hold
- a possible new scaffold skill upstream of `aoa-quest-harvest`
- patch-ready `aoa-skills` surfaces if one later bounded landing chooses them
- preserving the difference between session donor harvest and final quest
  promotion verdict

## What this bundle is not for

- no automatic owner-repo landing from `Dionysus`
- no claim that `aoa-techniques` lineage already exists
- no silent widening of `aoa-quest-harvest`
- no first-authoring into `aoa-routing` or `aoa-kag`
- no treatment of reviewed session residue as memory canon by default
- no conversion of this donor bundle into current `aoa-skills` truth without an
  explicit owner-repo choice

## Application posture

Use this donor note when one later planting needs to answer:

1. should `aoa-skills` accept `aoa-session-donor-harvest` as a scaffold skill
   before the new technique lineage is published
2. should the two pending technique IDs be published first in `aoa-techniques`
3. how should `aoa-quest-harvest` stay narrow once the broader donor-harvest
   skill exists

Until those answers are chosen in the owner repos, keep this bundle as bounded
donor input rather than treating it as an immediate merge order.

## Final rule

Preserve this bundle because it names a real adjacent skill seam that does not
yet exist upstream.

Do not confuse "patch-ready" with "already owner-approved."
