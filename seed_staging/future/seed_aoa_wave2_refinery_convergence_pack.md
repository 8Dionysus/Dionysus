# Seed Note: AoA Wave 2 Refinery Convergence Pack

## Purpose

Prepare the next downstream Growth Refinery convergence wave as one bounded
future prep surface inside `Dionysus`.

This pack preserves the second downstream layer after first-wave lineage is
already explicit and the remaining proof-gap closeout is already landed.

It does not invent a new sovereign layer. It hardens the existing downstream
cycle across derived funnel, bounded proof, recurring method, and bounded
writeback.

## Why this belongs in Dionysus

`Dionysus` is the seed garden and dispatch layer.
This prep pack belongs here because:

- the source artifact is a cross-repo convergence packet, not one owner-repo
  patch
- the live owner homes already exist, but the convergence work is not yet
  landed as one bounded rollout
- the bundle includes patch guides, synthetic fixtures, and validator ideas
  that should be adapted into repo-native patterns rather than copied blindly
- the route spans four owner layers and one shared audit seam
- the pack explicitly names negative rules that must stay visible before any
  planting starts

## Downstream posture to preserve

Read this pack under these downstream boundaries:

- keep first-wave lineage order explicit and unchanged:
  `cluster_ref -> candidate_ref -> seed_ref -> object_ref`
- keep downstream order explicit:
  `aoa-stats -> aoa-evals -> aoa-playbooks -> aoa-memo`
- keep `aoa-stats` derived-only and reviewed-only
- keep `aoa-evals` on bounded split proof bundles rather than one meta-proof
- keep `aoa-playbooks` on recurring method and review-gated automation seed
- keep `aoa-memo` on bounded failure/recovery recall only
- keep all downstream layers subordinate to owner truth

## Ownership posture

Read later landings under these owner boundaries:

- `aoa-stats` is the first honest owner for funnel hardening
- `aoa-evals` is the first honest owner for proof-family materialization
- `aoa-playbooks` is the first honest owner for `session-growth-cycle`
  surfacing and one review-gated automation seed entry
- `aoa-memo` is the first honest owner for writeback hardening
- a shared validator may later live in `Agents-of-Abyss` or another explicit
  shared audit home, but only after the owner-repo slices are real
- `Dionysus` preserves the pack and rollout posture only; it does not move
  owner truth here

## Application posture

Treat this pack as a staged downstream convergence source.

The honest landing route, if chosen later, is:

1. harden `aoa-stats` with richer reviewed-lineage input and no-overread tests
2. materialize support surfaces for the three existing Growth Refinery eval
   bundles
3. surface `session-growth-cycle` more clearly and add one review-gated
   automation seed plus one harvest or gate-review artifact
4. harden `aoa-memo` failure and recovery writeback examples and guardrails
5. add one cross-repo validator and audit pass

When planted later, regenerate generated surfaces from repo-local builders
instead of copying pack-local artifacts into live repos.

## What this pack is not for

- no new lineage order
- no new routing authority
- no new KAG authority
- no one total growth score
- no raw checkpoint-note ingestion into stats
- no hidden autonomous runner
- no memo as lineage ledger
- no direct import of pack-local fixtures or validator scripts as canonical
  truth

## Final rule

Keep this pack because it names the next downstream hardening cycle after
first-wave lineage and the explicit proof-gap closeout.

But preserve the difference between:

- a staged convergence pack in `Dionysus`
- derived funnel hardening in `aoa-stats`
- bounded proof-family materialization in `aoa-evals`
- recurring route surfacing in `aoa-playbooks`
- bounded recall and writeback hardening in `aoa-memo`
