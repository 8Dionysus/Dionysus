# Seed Note: AoA Antifragility Wave 4.5 Via Negativa Prep Pack

## Purpose

Prepare the `aoa-antifragility-wave-4-5-via-negativa-seed-2026-04-08` bundle
as one bounded inspect-first pruning pack inside `Dionysus`.

This pack preserves the subtraction posture for the antifragility stack after
waves one through four: reduce duplicate meaning, shadow authority, stale
convenience, and low-evidence permanence before inventing more protective
surface area.

## Why this belongs in Dionysus

`Dionysus` is the seed garden and dispatch layer.
This prep pack belongs here because:

- the source artifact is a repo-relative overlay seed, not a live owner-repo
  rollout
- the package spans the ecosystem center, product edge, reusable practice,
  bounded execution, routing, memo, agents, playbooks, KAG, proof, stats, SDK,
  and runtime substrate at once
- the machine-readable deletion candidate list is inspect-first and owner-aware,
  so it should be preserved as lineage rather than mistaken for an executable
  deletion order
- the seed explicitly prefers merge, move, suppress, quarantine, or deprecate
  before delete
- the seed says owner-local source-of-truth rules win when they are stricter

## Boundary posture to preserve

Read this pack under these boundaries:

- keep via negativa inspect-first, not mass-delete-first
- keep source-owned meaning in owner repos
- keep receipts, provenance anchors, and bounded proof stronger than cleanup
  aesthetics
- keep one-in-one-out as a forcing function against sprawl, not as a blind
  quota
- keep the center on constitutional rules and route maps rather than repo-local
  commands
- keep routing, memo, stats, KAG, SDK, and runtime helpers from seizing policy
  while "cleaning up"

## Ownership posture

Read later landings under these owner boundaries:

- `Agents-of-Abyss` owns via negativa doctrine, anti-authority rules, and
  one-in-one-out constitutional posture
- each touched owner repo owns its own pruning checklist and the actual
  merge/move/suppress/deprecate/delete decisions that survive review
- `Dionysus` only preserves the bundle, sequencing posture, and later lineage
  trail

## Application posture

Treat this pack as staged wave guidance until real owner-repo landings exist.

The honest path, if chosen next, is:

1. land the center doctrine and machine-readable inspect-first candidates
2. inspect live owner repos against those candidates
3. adapt repo-local pruning checklists to each repo's existing source-of-truth
   rules
4. only then decide whether any actual merge, move, suppress, quarantine,
   deprecate, or delete actions are earned

When planted later, adapt the via-negativa guidance to live owner-repo
conventions rather than copying the overlay seed blindly.

## What this pack is not for

- no mass deletion order from `Dionysus`
- no automatic file deletion
- no central cleanup empire
- no excuse for convenience layers to seize authority while pruning
- no deletion of source receipts, provenance anchors, or bounded proof artifacts
  just to satisfy a quota
- no ornamental minimalism that breaks operator workflows

## Final rule

Keep this pack because it names the subtraction posture the antifragility stack
now needs after four additive waves.

But preserve the difference between:

- inspect-first candidate prompts
- owner-local pruning judgments
- constitutional anti-authority rules
- one-in-one-out pressure against sprawl
- one staged via-negativa pack in `Dionysus`
