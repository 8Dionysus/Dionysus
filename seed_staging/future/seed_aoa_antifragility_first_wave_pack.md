# Seed Note: AoA Antifragility First-Wave Prep Pack

## Purpose

Prepare the `aoa-antifragility-first-wave-seed` bundle as one bounded
cross-repo prep pack inside `Dionysus`.

This note now preserves that first-wave family as landed lineage, including
the original prep posture and the later owner-repo merges, without turning
`Dionysus` into the owner of doctrine, source receipts, proof, or derived
vector meaning.

## Why this belongs in Dionysus

`Dionysus` is the seed garden and dispatch layer.
This prep pack belongs here because:

- the source artifact is a repo-relative overlay seed, not a live multi-repo
  patchset
- the original staging bundle spanned the ecosystem center, a concrete source
  owner, and several sibling AoA owner layers at once
- the intended `ATM10-Agent` owner landing had to go through GitHub rather
  than the `/srv` sibling-workspace path
- the original owner-repo reality check found no current antifragility traces
  in the checked AoA repos before the later merges
- the prep pack should stay replayable as lineage even after the owner
  landings merged upstream

## Boundary posture to preserve

Read this pack under these boundaries:

- keep source repos as the owners of meaning
- keep degraded modes valid, reviewable, and machine-readable
- keep `aoa-stats` derived rather than sovereign
- keep source-owned receipts, eval-owned proof, and stats-owned derived views
  distinct
- keep automation bounded and explicit rather than hidden
- keep antifragility split across multiple axes instead of one sovereign score

## Ownership posture

Read later landings under these owner boundaries:

- `Agents-of-Abyss` owns doctrine and first-wave scope language
- `ATM10-Agent` owns the first concrete stressor family, stressor receipts, and
  adaptation deltas
- `aoa-techniques` owns portable recovery and validation practice
- `aoa-evals` owns bounded proof and report shape
- `aoa-stats` owns derived vector summaries only
- `aoa-skills` owns the authoring-side addendum for fallback and receipt
  posture
- `Dionysus` only preserves the pack and its lineage posture

## What this pack is not for

- no immediate owner-repo rollout from `Dionysus`
- no assumption that `ATM10-Agent` should be planted from the current `/srv`
  sibling workflow
- no authority drift into `aoa-stats`
- no hidden automation widening or auto-remediation
- no single antifragility prestige score
- no treatment of later waves as current queue truth before first wave lands

## Final rule

Keep this pack because it names the first explicit antifragility posture family
across the public AoA surface.

But preserve the difference between:

- source-owned receipts
- eval-owned proof
- stats-owned derived views
- one preserved first-wave pack in `Dionysus`
