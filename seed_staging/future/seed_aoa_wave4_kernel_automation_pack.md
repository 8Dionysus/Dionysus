# Seed Note: AoA Wave 4 Kernel Automation Pack

## Purpose

Prepare the next Growth Refinery wave as one bounded future prep surface
inside `Dionysus`.

This pack preserves one governed session-growth kernel cycle after the earlier
lineage, owner-followthrough, and downstream owner surfaces are already
visible.

It does not invent a new scheduler layer. It hardens reviewed-closeout
signaling, remaining kernel examples, one reviewed automation route, and
derived summaries without replacing owner truth.

## Why this belongs in Dionysus

`Dionysus` is the seed garden and dispatch layer.
This prep pack belongs here because:

- the source artifact is a cross-repo kernel-automation packet, not one owner
  patch
- the live owner homes already exist, but the bounded reviewed kernel route is
  not yet landed as one explicit rollout
- the bundle includes patch guides, fixtures, and validator ideas that should
  be adapted into repo-native patterns rather than copied blindly
- the route spans four owner layers and one later shared audit seam
- the pack explicitly names negative rules around scheduler authority and
  silent mutation that must stay visible before planting begins

## Boundary posture to preserve

Read this pack under these route boundaries:

- keep the earlier lineage chain explicit and unchanged:
  `cluster_ref -> candidate_ref -> seed_ref -> object_ref`
- keep the wave route explicit:
  `aoa-sdk -> aoa-skills -> aoa-playbooks -> aoa-stats`
- keep `aoa-sdk` on deterministic reviewed-closeout recommendation and carry
  only
- keep `aoa-skills` on bounded packet and receipt meaning
- keep `aoa-playbooks` on reviewed automation follow-through, not schedules or
  hidden loops
- keep `aoa-stats` derived-only and weaker than owner truth, proof, approval,
  and review gates
- keep silent mutation, implicit approval, and scheduler authority out of the
  route

## Ownership posture

Read later landings under these owner boundaries:

- `aoa-sdk` is the first honest owner for deterministic kernel signal rules
  and the reviewed-closeout decision surface
- `aoa-skills` is the first honest owner for the remaining kernel packet and
  receipt examples plus maturity framing
- `aoa-playbooks` is the first honest owner for the reviewed automation
  follow-through scenario
- `aoa-stats` is the first honest owner for branch and automation
  follow-through summaries
- `Agents-of-Abyss` or another explicit shared audit home may later own one
  cross-repo validator, but only after the owner slices are real
- `Dionysus` preserves the pack and rollout posture only; it does not move
  owner truth here

## Application posture

Treat this pack as staged future prep work.

The honest landing order, if chosen later, is:

1. land `aoa-sdk` kernel signal rules
2. land `aoa-skills` remaining packet and receipt examples
3. land `aoa-playbooks` reviewed automation follow-through
4. land `aoa-stats` branch and follow-through summaries
5. add one explicit shared validator or audit pass

When planted later, regenerate generated surfaces from repo-local builders
instead of copying pack-local fixtures into live repos.

## What this pack is not for

- no scheduler truth
- no hidden autonomous runner
- no `candidate_ref`, `seed_ref`, or `object_ref` minting in `aoa-sdk`
- no playbook seed turned into a live scheduler registry
- no stats surfaces acting as routing or proof authority
- no repair or automation packet implying silent mutation or implicit approval
- no blind import of pack-local fixtures or tools

## Final rule

Keep this pack because it names the next bounded kernel-automation route after
the earlier lineage and owner-followthrough layers are already explicit.

But preserve the difference between:

- a staged kernel-automation pack in `Dionysus`
- reviewed-closeout recommendation in `aoa-sdk`
- bounded execution meaning in `aoa-skills`
- reviewed automation method in `aoa-playbooks`
- derived branch and follow-through views in `aoa-stats`
